Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any coding experience?
No! The sessions are designed for complete beginners. We use "vibe coding" techniques where you describe what you want in plain English, and AI tools help create the code. By Session 3, you'll understand how things work, but you won't need to write code from scratch.
What equipment do I need?
Just a laptop/desktop with internet access and a modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge). All software is free and cloud-based - nothing to install. A GitHub account (free for educators) is required.
Can sessions be delivered remotely?
Yes! Sessions are available in-person or via Zoom/Teams. Remote sessions include screen sharing, breakout rooms for practice, and recorded sessions for later review.
How long until I can create resources independently?
Most educators create their first simple resource during Session 2. By the end of Session 3, you'll be confident creating complete apps. With practice, you can build sophisticated resources in 2-3 hours.
Are resources accessible and GDPR compliant?
Yes! We emphasize accessibility best practices (WCAG 2.1) and GDPR compliance. Resources work offline, store no personal data, and can be hosted entirely within your school's infrastructure.
What ongoing support is included?
All packages include email support. Complete Programme includes 90 days support plus monthly Q&A sessions. Department packages include 6 months support and quarterly follow-ups. We also provide access to a growing library of templates and examples.
Can I share resources with other teachers?
Absolutely! That's the beauty of GitHub. You can share links, allow colleagues to copy and adapt your work, or collaborate on projects together. We encourage building a community of educator-developers.
What subjects/ages is this suitable for?
Any subject, any age group! Examples in training cover Maths, English, Science, but the techniques apply universally. From Primary (interactive games) to A-Level (complex revision tools), the only limit is your imagination.
